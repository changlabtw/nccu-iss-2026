NCCU International Summer School 2026

Digital Communication and Emerging Technologies

Emerging Technologies in AI: From Foundations to the Frontier of Science

July 20 – July 28, 2026 · 09:10–12:00 · Instructor: Jia-Ming Chang, Dept. of Computer Science, NCCU · No programming background required.

Welcome. This is the course hub: your daily schedule, readings, and all course documents in one place. Each morning runs 09:10–10:30 lecture, a 10-minute break, then a hands-on computer-lab session in the browser from about 10:45 to 12:00 (no installation, no personal laptop needed). We suggest Claude for consistency, but you're welcome to use any AI tool you like (ChatGPT, Gemini, and others).

Before Day 1: set up free accounts for a Google account (for Colab and NotebookLM) and Claude (claude.ai). We do this together in the first session, but arriving with accounts ready saves time.

Daily schedule

DateTopic & in-class activityKey readings
7/20
Mon		 AI: The Big Picture & Neural Network Intuition. Build intuition for how a neural network learns with TensorFlow Playground (no coding). Form teams; receive the project brief. Gates (2023), on AI agents · Hopfield (1982), PNAS · TensorFlow Playground
7/21
Tue		 Deep Learning in Action. Train an image classifier (Fashion-MNIST) in a ready-made Colab notebook; experiment with parameters and overfitting. 3Blue1Brown, "But what is a neural network?" · Krizhevsky et al. (2012) · Google Colab
7/22
Wed		 Generative AI, LLMs & AI Agents. Transformers, foundation models, embeddings. Lab: prompt engineering in Colab (free model via OpenRouter); pick your final-project topic and draft its system prompt. Days 3–5 build one project: a chatbot that answers from your own documents (RAG). Generative AI for Beginners · Vaswani et al. (2017), "Attention Is All You Need" · Prompt Engineering course
7/23
Thu		 AI at the Frontier of Science. AlphaGo, AlphaFold, and the 2024 Nobel Prizes. Lab: build a RAG system so your project chatbot answers from your own texts. Nobel Prize in Chemistry 2024 · Jumper et al. (2021), AlphaFold · The Founder's Playbook
7/27
Mon		 Build & Ship Your AI App (Gradio). Wrap your RAG system in a shareable web chat app with Gradio; upload your texts, get a public link, and assemble the final project (app + poster) for Day 6. Gradio, "Creating a Chatbot Fast" · Lewis et al. (2020), RAG · OpenRouter
7/28
Tue		 Group Presentations. Teams present a chosen AI application or issue, with Q&A, peer feedback, and course wrap-up. Anderson (2013), HBR · Bourne (2007), "Ten Simple Rules"
